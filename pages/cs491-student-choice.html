GloveOne: At a Glance

As of yet we have explored VR technology that lets us see virtual reality, hear sounds. But is that the extent of what we can do with VR? What about tactile sense? Imagine punching a wall in VR and actually feeling the wall resist your fist. Imagine the vibrations that it would send down your arm and body. Imagine if you could also the feel the sting in your bones that comes with punching the wall?

With GloveOne, this can become a possibility. GloveOne is a tool that lets you use both hands to feel virtual reality. Now you can see a butterfly land on your hand, you can hear it (not that I know of butterflies to make much sound), and you can feel the small vibrations it makes when it flutters its wings.

Armed with 10 haptic actuators and a conductive fabric, the glove allows both gesture movement to be tracked and allows sensation in the hand. This is a step forward in terms of achieving full virtual reality.
