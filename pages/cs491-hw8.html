Student’s Choice Presentation Critique 1

Project: Neurable: control VR with your mind

By Giovanni Monna

Neurable VR presents a tool that can be essentially controlled by your brain. How? Through the neural impulses that occur when you make a decision consciously or unconsciously. It is within this very statement that lies Neurable’s greatest challenge.

The tool is controlled by impulses from the brain. These impulses are something that humans by nature do not have control over. So in a controlled setting, where the impulses are honed and limited in understanding by the tool, it is something that can be used well. However, even then, it seems more of an experimental tool than something that can serve an actual purpose. This is not to say that it should be abandoned and not produced. After all, such tools are intermediary steps for bigger more impactful tools to come in the future.

In the case that neural impulses are misinterpreted or even mishandled, things could go wrong. Or in the case that certain impulses are not registered. Alternatively, certain impulses can be triggered involuntarily by the brain. As I mentioned earlier, humans do not have full control over their neural impulses, and so a subconscious impulse may lead to the tool doing something unpredictable and unwanted.
