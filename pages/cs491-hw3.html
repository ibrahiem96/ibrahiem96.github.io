HW 3: Scaling in VR

        

As you can see from the image above, the scaling affects the way we users perceive the VR environment. In essence I noticed two main changes. First is that the movement became immensely more difficult. It was cumbersome to constantly move what seemed a long distance but in reality was getting you nowhere near your desired destination. I exaggerate, but instead of pointing and teleporting to a location I know had to complete the movement in 3-4 points.

The second thing is obviously the texture and pixelation changes. Much of the room was now very pixelated because of how close we are to the objects.         This makes it less visually appealing and less realistic.

The last thing is object manipulation. Of course, at this close, manipulating the coffee mug on the table required more than just careful maneuvering of the controllers, but it was often difficult to move it very much. This is because even what seemed like large movements to me in reality, were actually made miniscule tenfold in virtual reality. Throwing the coffee mug in reality actually only moved it a few inches in virtual reality.
