What is GloveOne

Created by NeuroDigital Technologies in Spain, the Gloveone is a VR tool which allows users to feel the sensation of touch. How does it accomplish this? Through the use of 10 haptic sensors it applies forces, vibrations, and motions. It also has conductive fabric that is used for motion tracking and gesture control. These are two main technologies within the gloveone that allow it to simulate touch.

Background

As of now, we have VR technology that caters to our senses of sight and sound. We have technology that maps to our depth perception, colors, and movements. We have technology that provides 3d sounds that is also immersive. However, with the current technology we have only seen touch feedback in the form of simple vibrations. Although this is fine for games, other applications can be enhanced with full touch sensations. Moreover, some applications of virtual reality may even require touch sensation.

Features

The Gloveone comes with many cool features which I will discuss below:

Firstly, let’s discuss what the Gloveone is capable of doing:

  • Weigh virtual objects
  • Interact with buttons and elements
  • Differentiate textures
  • Feel the sound waves
  • Receive haptic warnings and alert events
  • Trigger actions and commands like a smart controller
  • Feel the soft fluttering of a butterfly
  • Feel the rain drops Impact or the intensity of a virtual fire
  • Feel the impact of smashing/crushing object

Imagine touching sandpaper and feeling its texture. Imagine tossing an apple between your hands, and feelings its weight, texture, and shape. Imagine feeling the heat of a fire. All these are now possible with the Gloveone.

Now, let’s move on to the technical features of the Gloveone:

Communication

  • Low latency Bluetooth 4.0
  • Ultra low latency mode with USB

Motion Tracking

  • Hand orientation with 1 IMU (9-Axis)

Functionality

  • 10 vibrotactile actuators
  • Contact areas for digital and reliable gesture recognition
  • Extremely low footprint for PC and mobile devices

Others

  • Long life Li-Po Battery

Along with these, the GloveOne comes with 5 flex sensors (one on each finger) for precise finger tracking.

Design

As shown above in the pictures, the Gloveone is a simple slip-on glove, that can work with most modern VR gear (shown in the example above with VIVE Headset). The design is simple and seems very easy to use. Moreover, it is made simply so that it can be affordable for users.
