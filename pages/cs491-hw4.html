VR Office Reviews

Sandeep Rane

As you can see from the above screenshots, this office is well designed and it is well thought out. I liked how it was split into two parts, one designed to be a waiting area to greet clients and customers and one designed to be where the employees get work done. The placement and scale of the objects is both accurate and realistic. Although at certain angles it seems that things are placed too close together at times. For example, the space between the bean bags and the three desk/cubicle seems very narrow. However, if you change the angles it looks as if there’s enough room to walk in between. This made me think about how we perceive space in reality. Even in reality objects that seem very close together are in fact sometimes far apart. It is just our viewing angle. So then why is it disturbing? Perhaps because this is VR and our eyes cannot adjust to it? Its not that our eyes know that this is VR and so it is not reality. Our eyes are used to the motion and perception that reality provides (which is not completely emulated in VR). There are far more viewing angles in VR and you can zoom into objects at a much greater degree. I believe since our eyes are used to reality, that when they notice these differences in VR they in effect are signalling to our brain that something is off. Anyways, back to the office at hand. I have few complaints against it. Perhaps some of the colors could have been chosen better, it is a bit dreary. I don’t particularly like the wall that separates the two parts of the office. For one, it is not even. Secondly, I wish it was plain glass all around, this makes it seem unsightly. Lastly, it should not be glass, or at least only the top should be glass.

This second office utilizes there space in a different way. The first thing you’ll notice is that there is a lot more open space in this office. You might think that after reviewing the first office I would find this office more appealing since I disliked the narrowness of the previous room. However, I find that the scale is a little off. What I mean by this is that it his hard (using just the keys at least) to scale ourselves down to human size. Therefore if you look at the very last picture in the grid, there just seems too much space in between the desks. Imagine entering from the door in that last picture. It would be very odd given how far apart the desks are. One solution to this is to add more things. However, I don’t think that is a very good solution because the human eye can still perceive that something is off. Why? Because the scale is not right. The tables should be made a little larger in my opinion and the distance between them should be shortened a little. One other thing I found strange was the desks themselves. Working that close together is not comfortable at all. You have to think about the way people sit and how big they are and how their hands and feet will move around and also about how they feel working that close together. This is an error employers sometimes make I believe. To enhance productivity or ot cut back on costs they just group together things that can be grouped and toss them into the office without really thinking about the people using them. Just because the desks can be grouped like that doesn’t mean they should.

Good things: I really liked the animated aquarium wall. Some of the wall art is kind of creepy.
