Discussion of VR Feel

        Obviously, the VR room (no matter how much time I had spent on it) will not be able to replicate reality (at least not as of right now).

In VR, its very easy to go through walls, floors, ceilings. Its very easy to place an object through a wall or floor. It makes the physics of objects seem unrealistic (some things bounce too much, or fly too far when you throw them). Objects that are likely to break in reality don't break in VR.

In terms of size, the room although in measurement the same, feels smaller. Yet at the same time at certain areas it feels larger. I would pin this in the scaling and zooming that can be done in VR. Certain objects may have slightly incorrects scaling--I feel as if the human eye is perceptive of that. Zooming into a corner of the room will fill your vision with that corner. In reality, I would have to be inches away from the corner to feel the same feeling.
