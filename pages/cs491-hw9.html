Student’s Choice Presentation Critique 2

Project: SketchAR 

By: Vasanth Pandiarajan

SketchAR presents an interesting concept, however, not a new one. In most cases a tool which emulates physical drawing is limited by feel. The feeling of pressing on paper or some other medium and feeling the strokes of your hand. The feeling of having control over you strokes to make precise and accurate motions. These feelings are difficult to emulate perfectly for most VR or computer graphic tools.

This is not to say that the human body can never be in tune with using these tools to draw or paint. It is simply a fact that most people still develop their drawing/writing on physical paper. Therefore, adjusting to drawing in VR becomes not impossible, but difficult. Now, there are a number of ways in which these tools can make this adjustment easier. You can emulate the physical hardness of a paper or other medium. This can be done by providing feedback in the drawing tool to help the artist/drawer feel things like pressure, flatness of the tip, ink, etc.

In regards to this, SketchAR is a much better tool. It uses augmented reality to help the artist draw on a physical medium. That is, the medium still remains physical, but you have the added assistance of an augmented reality to guide your hand.

One criticism I do have of SketchAR is that it may at times be prove to be a hindrance rather than an assistance. Imagine you have your sketch outlined in front of you, and you wish to paint over the sketch, using it as an outline. Would it not be annoying to see your outline despite painting over it? To me, it cause confusion and annoyance, something that I would grow to dislike over time.
